Frequently Asked Questions

What does $20 get me?

Your $20 buys you:
  • A position in the queue
  • Up to 1 hour of FogSift's focused attention on your submission
  • A PDF write-up and a video response scoped to your submission

A full-on research squad (me + AI + friends + network) focused on your submission for up to an hour. $20 is the accessibility filter that keeps it serious and keeps it open to anyone who can get a job at McDonald's. Worth way more than $20, kept low so anyone can access it.

We may or may not publish a video. Your submission may be used for other purposes, including marketing. Request anonymization if needed.

What do you mean "a position in the queue"?

When you submit your problem, it enters the queue. You can see all pending submissions there, along with recently completed ones.

Every day, FogSift selects items from the queue to work on. Your position tells you roughly where you are in line, but selection isn't strictly first-come-first-served. More on that below.

What do you mean "up to 1 hour"? I don't get the full hour?

You get up to the full hour.

Every day, we choose two items from the queue:

  • One from the top - the highest priority item
  • One at random - keeps it interesting

Each submission gets up to 1 hour of focused attention. At or before the hour mark, we stop working and begin drafting your PDF write-up and video response.

Sometimes we crack it in 20 minutes. Sometimes the full hour isn't enough. Either way, you get what we found.

Can you refuse to work on my submission?

Yes. FogSift reserves the right to refuse any submission for any reason at any time.

If we refuse your submission before starting work, we'll refund your $20. If we've already started and realize we can't help, we may or may not refund depending on how much time was invested.

This rarely happens. But we want to be upfront: if your problem is outside our wheelhouse or feels like a bad fit, we'll let you know.

Can I request a refund?

Yes. In fact, I encourage it.

I often find myself deeply involved in projects that consume all my focus. It may be a while before I get to your request.

  • If you're okay waiting: Your request is safe in the queue. It'll get worked on eventually.
  • If it's time-sensitive: No problem! Request a refund and resubmit when timing is better. Or pay to bump your position (see below).

Can I pay to move up in the queue?

Yes. There's a daily "bump" option.

Here's how it works:

  • Once daily, all submitters can pay a one-time fee to bump their submission up in the queue
  • This prevents abuse. People with deep pockets can't just buy out the entire queue
  • It keeps things fair while giving time-sensitive requests a path forward

Think of it as "expedited shipping" for your problem.

What if I want to work with FogSift more?

Great! We want to hear from you.

The best way to start is the same as everyone else: submit your problem for $20. In your submission, mention that you're interested in deeper engagement.

If your submission is selected ahead of schedule, or if you pay the daily bump fee, we'll connect sooner.

This is my best attempt to make incoming requests fair and accessible to as many people as possible as I scale.

If you made it this far, you're exactly the kind of person we want to work with. You pay attention. You do the homework. You don't skip to the end.

That's rare. And it matters.
