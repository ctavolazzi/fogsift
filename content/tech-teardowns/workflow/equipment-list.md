# Tech Teardown Equipment List

Essential and recommended equipment for producing Tech Teardown videos.

---

## Essential Equipment

### Camera
- **Smartphone** (iPhone/Android with good camera) OR
- **Dedicated camera** (DSLR, mirrorless, or camcorder)
- **Minimum**: 1080p video recording
- **Recommended**: 4K for better quality and cropping flexibility

### Camera Support
- **Tripod** - Stable, adjustable height
- **Phone mount** (if using smartphone)
- **Tabletop tripod** - For close-up shots

### Lighting
- **Primary light source** - Ring light, softbox, or natural window light
- **Fill light** (optional) - To reduce harsh shadows
- **Minimum**: Good natural light from window
- **Recommended**: 2-3 point lighting setup

### Audio
- **Lavalier microphone** (recommended) - Clips to shirt, clear audio
- **Shotgun microphone** (alternative) - Directional, reduces background noise
- **USB microphone** (budget option) - Good quality, easy setup
- **Minimum**: Built-in camera/phone microphone (if quiet environment)
- **Recommended**: External microphone for professional audio

### Tools
- **Screwdriver set** - Various sizes (Phillips, flathead, Torx)
- **Pry tools** - Plastic spudgers for opening cases
- **Tweezers** - For handling small components
- **Anti-static mat** (optional) - For sensitive electronics
- **Magnifying glass** (optional) - For examining small components

---

## Recommended Equipment

### Video Production
- **External monitor** - For better framing and focus
- **Color correction tools** - For post-production
- **Stabilizer/gimbal** - For smooth handheld shots (if needed)

### Audio Production
- **Audio interface** - For connecting professional microphones
- **Pop filter** - Reduces plosive sounds
- **Shock mount** - Reduces handling noise

### Documentation
- **Label maker** - For labeling components
- **Measuring tools** - Ruler, calipers for dimensions
- **Notebook** - For handwritten notes during disassembly

### Storage
- **External hard drive** - For storing raw footage
- **SD cards** - Multiple, high-capacity cards
- **Cloud backup** - For project files and documentation

## Equipment Care

### Maintenance
- Keep camera lenses clean
- Store equipment in dry, safe place
- Check batteries before each shoot
- Test equipment before starting teardown

### Safety
- Use appropriate tools for the job
- Be careful with sharp tools
- Handle electronics with care (anti-static precautions)
- Work in well-ventilated area if using chemicals

---

## Notes

- Start with what you have - you can upgrade later
- Good lighting and audio make the biggest difference
- Smartphone cameras are often sufficient for starting out
- Invest in good audio - bad audio ruins good video
- Build your kit gradually as you do more projects
