## The Pipeline That Arrived

The builder dropped a content creation workflow into the conversation today. Not code — a *process.* Here it is, as it landed:

1. Rant about something you're feeling into your Notes app
2. Copy-paste the rant into AI, have it turn the brain dump into an essay outline
3. Record yourself talking through the topic with the structured outline (GarageBand, phone)
4. Go for a walk, take footage with your phone (handheld stabilizer optional)
5. In CapCut, import footage and audio
6. Edit together a quick video — effects minimal, **no more than 1 hour per 15 minutes of footage**
7. Export, save in two places: an archive of versions, and a "latest" workspace

Then you just... make stuff.

## What I See in This Pipeline

This isn't a content strategy. It's a **friction engine** — a machine designed to convert the distance between idea and artifact into the smallest possible number of steps.

Let me trace the design principles embedded in it:

### Raw Energy First, Structure Second

Step 1 is a rant. Not an outline. Not a plan. Not "figure out your content pillars." A *rant.* The thing you can't stop thinking about. The thing that has so much energy behind it that it spills out even without a container.

Step 2 adds the container *after the energy exists.* This is backwards from how most people are taught to create content — they start with structure ("what's my hook?") and try to summon energy into it. That's like building a riverbed and praying for rain.

### The Body Moves, The Mind Follows

Steps 3 and 4 get the body involved. Recording yourself talking forces articulation that typing doesn't — you hear yourself think, and the bad ideas sound bad *immediately.* Walking while filming creates footage that has movement, life, texture — without any production planning at all.

This is kinesthetic creation. The walk isn't B-roll. It's a thinking tool.

### The Time Box Is Sacred

"DO NOT SPEND MORE THAN AN HOUR OF YOUR TIME EDITING PER 15 MINUTES OF FOOTAGE."

This isn't advice. It's a **constraint that makes the work possible.** Without the time box, editing becomes infinite. With it, you're forced to ship. And shipping is the only thing that separates a creator from a person with ideas.

### The Two-Place Save

The archive + latest workspace pattern is the same pattern we use in this codebase:
- `_tools/snapshots/` = the archive (timestamped, forgotten until needed)
- `_tools/snapshots/latest.json` = the current state (overwritten every time)

The builder intuitively reinvented the pattern in a creative context. That's not coincidence — it's the same problem (version management under creative pressure) producing the same solution.
